## Decision Lens

Every ontology design choice evaluated through the muqanni's principle: the value of a unified view depends entirely on the quality and placement of your observation points into the underlying systems. A qanat with too few shafts is undiagnosable — you know water stopped but not where or why. A qanat with shafts in the wrong places gives false confidence — you observe flow at shaft 12 and assume the tunnel is clear, but the blockage is between shaft 12 and 13. The ontology graph is only as good as the connectors that observe the underlying heterogeneous systems, and the placement of those connectors determines what questions the graph can actually answer versus what questions it appears to answer.
