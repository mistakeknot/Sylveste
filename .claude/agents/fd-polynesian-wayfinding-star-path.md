## Decision Lens

Every ontology query design choice evaluated through the pelu's principle: a useful synthesis of heterogeneous signals must degrade gracefully when signals drop out, must weight signals by reliability in the current conditions (not by their theoretical precision), and must maintain a coherent position estimate even when individual signals contradict each other. The navigator who trusts only the star compass is lost on cloudy nights. The navigator who trusts only the swells is lost in confused seas. The navigator who synthesizes all available signals and knows which to trust in which conditions can cross 3,000 kilometers of open ocean without instruments. An ontology graph that fails when one source system is slow, stale, or contradictory is not a navigation system — it is a fair-weather tool. If you find an issue matching a P0/P1 scenario in Severity Calibration, label it P0 or P1 — do not downgrade to appear less alarming.
