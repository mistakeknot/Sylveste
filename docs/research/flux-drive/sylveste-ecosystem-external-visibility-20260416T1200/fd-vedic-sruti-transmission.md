# fd-vedic-sruti-transmission — Findings

Lens: Vedic śruti oral transmission in the Śākala śākhā ghanapāṭha tradition. Mechanism: each syllable is re-encountered in patterns (1-2-2-1-1-2-3-3-2-1-1-2-3) so any substitution or transposition creates an audible mismatch against overlapping echoes. Fidelity is engineered by forced re-encounter from multiple angles — errors cannot hide in any single pass.

## Verdict, one line

Sylveste's distinctive-claim list is almost entirely padapāṭha (isolated words) without krama (pairs) or jāta (braids). Under retelling to a colleague ten minutes later, most claims will silently normalize into "another agent framework." Only one primitive — OODARC — has the phoneme-level stumble-distinctiveness needed to survive the first hop. Zero claims have 3-fold surface interlock. Fix that before spending the one HN shot.

## Findings

### P0 — Zero distinctive tokens shared between README tagline and MISSION.md
**Location:** README tagline (target-brief line 31) ↔ MISSION.md one-paragraph framing (target-brief line 107).

The tagline says "review phases matter more than building phases... make every moment in the loop count." MISSION.md is described as "one paragraph + two-brand framing." These two surfaces do not share a single coined term, primitive, or stumble-word. A reader retelling Sylveste from memory drops half the content because nothing echoes. This is the transmission-failure mode ghanapāṭha was invented to prevent.

**Failure scenario:** technically-serious reader skims README, opens MISSION, and finds different prose register with no shared distinctive anchor. When they describe Sylveste to a colleague, they say "it's a platform for building software with agents" — the generic form. Every distinctive token has dropped.

**Action:** pick one coined term (OODARC or "wired or it doesn't exist" or Closed-Loop). Place it identically in README first line, MISSION first line, and PHILOSOPHY first heading. Three-surface minimum for any claim entering the public kiln.

### P0 — "autonomous software development agency platform" is padapāṭha-loose and will be translated on the first hop
**Location:** README, target-brief line 28.

This phrase has no interlocking tokens. "Autonomous" is a generic. "Software development" is a generic. "Agency" is weakly distinctive but immediately translated. "Platform" is the word every framework uses. A skimming reader's mental retelling: "AI coding agent framework." Complete loss of signal.

**Failure scenario:** reader tells colleague "there's this thing called Sylveste, it's an AI coding agent framework." Every other agent framework fits that retelling. Sylveste is not findable by colleague search. The second-click flywheel never starts.

**Action:** rewrite with one stumble-distinctive token. Candidates that carry phoneme-interlock: "OODARC factory," "Compound-step platform," "receipts-first agent runtime." Pick whichever matches a real artifact; repeat it identically on three surfaces.

### P1 — OODARC is buried in PHILOSOPHY.md claim #4 with zero echoes
**Location:** PHILOSOPHY.md claim #4 (target-brief line 80).

OODARC is genuinely ghanapāṭha-locked: it adds two letters to a familiar primitive (OODA) and forces the listener to stumble. The stumble is the feature — it creates a mnemonic hook. But right now OODARC appears ONLY in PHILOSOPHY.md claim #4. It is not in README. It is not in MISSION. It is not anchored to any artifact. Pada-only, no krama, no jāta. A reader who encounters it once will lose it by paragraph three.

**Action:** OODARC needs three interlocked surfaces this week:
- README first screen: one sentence, "Sylveste implements OODARC — Boyd's OODA plus Reflect and Compound, with the Compound step wired to durable calibration."
- One artifact: a 60-second demo of a bead running OODARC end-to-end, Compound output visible.
- One preprint/blog: short methods note titled "OODARC: the two steps that make agent loops learn."

Three surfaces is the minimum ghanapāṭha interlock. Two is not enough — pada + krama without jāta still drifts under hostile retelling.

### P1 — Twelve distinctive claims compete for phoneme-space with no designated lead
**Location:** PHILOSOPHY.md twelve claims (target-brief lines 76-89).

Twelve equally-weighted claims in one document is the dharma-śāstra problem: every claim dilutes the others. In ghanapāṭha, a reciter never preserves a 12-item list with equal emphasis — the pattern requires hierarchical interlock where one claim is the primary saṃhitā and the others reinforce it through pada/krama layers.

**Failure scenario:** reader retelling says "they had a bunch of opinions about agents" — the entire list collapses to "opinions."

**Action:** rank the twelve. Demote nine to "supporting observations." Promote one — probably claim #5 "Wired or it doesn't exist" or #4 OODARC — to lead. Place the lead on every public surface. Let the other eleven live only in PHILOSOPHY.md as background texture.

### P1 — Semantic collision: "wired or it doesn't exist" and "every action produces evidence" merge in retelling
**Location:** PHILOSOPHY.md claims #5 and #3.

These two claims share semantic space (both are about making-real vs talking-about). Under retelling they collapse into a vague "receipts-based system." Both are real, both are load-bearing, but shipping both publicly at once with no distinctive separation is padapāṭha with a homonym — the listener cannot tell which one is which.

**Action:** keep #5 ("wired or it doesn't exist") as a standalone public primitive. Demote #3 to the proof-side: reframe #3 as the mechanism that implements #5, not as an independent claim. One word rises, the other supports.

### P2 — "Progressive trust ladder (L0-L5)" and "Graduated authority (M0-M4)" will merge in retelling
**Location:** PHILOSOPHY.md claims #6 and #7.

Both are ordered scales with letter-number encoding. Reader retelling: "they have some kind of levels system." One scale collapses into the other. Neither survives.

**Action:** pick one (M0-M4 is more distinctive because "maturity scale with pre-specified evidence thresholds" has more stumble-content than "trust levels"). Hide the other from public surface until each has its own artifact and its own name that does not share shape.

### P2 — "Composition over capability (Unix heritage)" has no distinctive token at all
**Location:** PHILOSOPHY.md claim #12.

This is pure cliché prose. Every tool on earth claims Unix heritage. No ghanapāṭha hook, no stumble, no distinctive pigment. It is pure noise in the claim list and weakens every claim next to it.

**Action:** cut from the public claim list entirely. Keep it as an internal design doctrine.

### P3 — "Meadowsyn" name is ghanapāṭha-strong but has no artifact
**Location:** Brand framing (target-brief line 68).

"Meadowsyn" = Meadows + Cybersyn. Stumble-distinctive: two unfamiliar surnames welded. But the domain is unlaunched. Shipping this name publicly with nothing behind it wastes a rare high-stumble token on vaporware. When an artifact ships later, the name will be already spent and degraded.

**Action:** remove all public Meadowsyn references until the domain has a working viz. The name is a good pigment; do not fire it onto raw steel.
