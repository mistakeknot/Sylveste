# Sylveste — External-Visibility Prioritization Synthesis

## The Single Question

Ruthless prioritization pathway to put Sylveste on the radar of the technically serious AI community — what to ship, polish, or cancel to maximize legibility and external signal rather than internal completeness. The brief explicitly constrains the answer: one HN post, one demo video, one blog post per week, one or two polished subsystems. Every reviewer was told to be subtractive.

## Executive Verdict

Sixteen agents across four semantic distances converge on the same answer with uncommon force: **cancel almost everything from the first-contact surface, promote a single artifact — the Closed-Loop cost-calibration pipeline (`estimate-costs.sh` + interstat actuals + `fleet-registry.yaml` calibration, tagged with the $2.93 / 785 sessions / March 2026 baseline) — to uchigumori polish, and sequence one embargo-lift day that pairs preprint + demo + HN post + tweet thread.** The single highest-leverage move this week is not the HN post, not the tagline rewrite, and not the preprint — it is turning `estimate-costs.sh` into a cron-driven, always-on public viewing-line at a stable URL (Track C's "canal"; Track D's "swell"; Track A's "receipt"). Everything else — Garden Salon, Meadowsyn, the three-layer × six-pillar table, 8 of the 12 distinctive claims in PHILOSOPHY.md, the 64-plugin enumeration, the track-level numerics (A≈L2, B≈L1, C≈L0), Ockham/Interweave/Interop/Skaffen/Zaka-Alwe/Autarch from any public-facing list — gets cut from first-contact surfaces until each carries its own receipt. The goal is not more Sylveste. It is the *visible* Sylveste.
